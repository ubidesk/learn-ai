# Mission & Product Requirements (PRD)

## North Star

Learn AI takes a complete beginner — someone who has never written a line
of code — all the way to becoming a thoughtful, capable, professional AI
engineer.

Every design and content decision must answer a single question:

> Will this help a complete beginner build a durable mental model and
> move toward professional AI engineering capability?

If the answer is no, it does not ship.

## Learner promise

- **Free forever.** No paywalls, no gated tiers, no upsells.
- **Open source.** Curriculum, code, and reasoning are public.
- **Honest.** We name what is hard, what is optional, and what is hype.
- **Complete.** From "what is a computer?" to shipping production AI
  systems — no gaps assumed away.
- **Made in public.** Progress, mistakes, and revisions are visible.

## Non-negotiables

1. Never assume prior programming, mathematics, computer science, or AI
   knowledge without teaching it first.
2. Motivation and intuition come before terminology and notation.
3. Every concept earns its keep with a concrete example and a
   learner-visible check for understanding.
4. Nothing is introduced that will not be used again — no orphan topics.
5. Accessibility, readability, and keyboard support are baseline, not
   polish.

## Out of scope for the current rebuild phase

- Authentication, accounts, and progress persistence.
- Payments and monetization.
- AI chat tutor, embeddings, or LLM-backed features.
- Any backend (Supabase / Lovable Cloud) integration.

The rebuild is content-first and public. Interactive infrastructure is
added only after the curriculum spine exists.
